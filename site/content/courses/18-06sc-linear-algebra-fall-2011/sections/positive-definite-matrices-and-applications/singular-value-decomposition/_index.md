Session Overview
----------------

| ![Figure excerpted from 'Introduction to Linear Algebra' by G.S. Strang](https://open-learning-course-data-ci.s3.amazonaws.com/18-06sc-linear-algebra-fall-2011/319b1b83d1cce88496ae7e7fb923aec4_3_5.jpg) | {{< br >}}{{< br >}}If _A_ is symmetric and positive definite, there is an orthogonal matrix _Q_ for which _A_ = _Q_Λ_QT_. Here Λ is the matrix of eigenvalues. Singular Value Decomposition lets us write _any_ matrix _A_ as a product _U_Σ_VT_ where _U_ and _V_ are orthogonal and Σ is a diagonal matrix whose non-zero entries are square roots of the eigenvalues of _ATA_. The columns of _U_ and _V_ give bases for the four fundamental subspaces.{{< br >}}{{< br >}}
